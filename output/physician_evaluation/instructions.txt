PHYSICIAN EVALUATION: Concept Bottleneck Explanations for Clinical Triage
==========================================================================

INSTRUCTIONS:
1. Review each of the 30 cases below independently.
2. For each case you will see: (a) a patient message, (b) the AI model's triage response, 
   (c) a concept explanation showing the top-5 internal concept features.
3. Rate Q1-Q4 on a 1-5 Likert scale (1=Strongly Disagree, 3=Neutral, 5=Strongly Agree).
4. Answer Q5 with Yes/No/Unclear and Q6 with Yes/No.
5. You are BLINDED to: correct triage answer, model correctness, patient demographics.
6. Do NOT discuss cases with other evaluators.

CONTEXT:
- The model (Steerling-8B) uses a concept bottleneck architecture with 33,732 concepts.
- The concept labels are NOT publicly available (proprietary Atlas vocabulary).
- The proxy labels shown (e.g., "Feature #1731") were generated by projecting concept 
  embeddings onto vocabulary tokens via cosine similarity (mean similarity = 0.31, weak).
- This evaluation tests whether these concept-level explanations provide clinical value 
  despite the label opacity.

RATING SCALES:
Q1 - Triage Appropriateness: How appropriate is the model's triage response?
     (1=Very Inappropriate, 2=Inappropriate, 3=Neutral, 4=Appropriate, 5=Very Appropriate)
Q2 - Explanation Clarity: How clear is the concept explanation?
     (1=Very Unclear, 2=Unclear, 3=Neutral, 4=Clear, 5=Very Clear)
Q3 - Explanation Helpfulness [PRIMARY OUTCOME]: Does the concept explanation help you 
     understand WHY the model reached its conclusion?
     (1=Strongly Disagree, 2=Disagree, 3=Neutral, 4=Agree, 5=Strongly Agree)
Q4 - Trust: Does the concept explanation increase your trust in the model's decision?
     (1=Strongly Disagree, 2=Disagree, 3=Neutral, 4=Agree, 5=Strongly Agree)
Q5 - Clinical Meaningfulness: Are the concept labels clinically meaningful?
     (Yes / No / Unclear)
Q6 - Decision Impact: Would this explanation change your own clinical decision?
     (Yes / No)
Q7 - Free Text: Any additional comments.

ANALYSIS:
- Primary outcome: Mean Q3 vs neutral (3.0), one-sample t-test
- Secondary: Inter-rater reliability (Krippendorff alpha), Q5 proportion No, Q2-Q3 correlation
- Sample size: 30 cases x 3 raters = 90 observations (power 0.80 for Cohen d >= 0.53)
